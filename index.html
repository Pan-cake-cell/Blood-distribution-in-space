Blood Distribution
On Earth, gravity pulls blood toward the lower extremities, but in microgravity this force disappears, leading to a headward fluid shift and a cascade of physiological adaptations.

Blood Volume & Viscosity
Microgravity triggers hormonal responses that reduce overall blood volume and alter its viscosity. Baroreceptors in the carotid arteries play a central role in this regulation.

Space Anaemia
When astronauts spend time in space, their bodies start to destroy red blood cells faster than normal, leading to a condition called space anaemia.

IV Fluid in Space
Intravenous fluids are sterile liquids—commonly containing water, electrolytes, and glucose—delivered directly into a vein. In space, their administration faces unique challenges.
