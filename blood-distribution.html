Blood Distribution

Module Introduction

On Earth, gravity pulls blood toward the lower extremities, but in microgravity this force disappears, leading to a headward fluid shift. As a result, more blood and other bodily fluids move toward the chest, neck, and head, giving astronauts a "puffy face" appearance and reducing volume in the legs.

This redistribution triggers physiological responses:
